# Problem Statement

## The Core Problem

Many women face persistent challenges when purchasing abayas due to the limited availability of sizes that accommodate diverse body types. Standardized sizing systems in the market often fail to account for individual variations in body proportions, resulting in poor fit, discomfort, and dissatisfaction.

This forces many customers to:

- Try on multiple garments before finding one that fits properly,
- Spend extra time and money on tailoring alterations, or
- Settle for ill-fitting abayas — leading to increased costs and wasted time.

## Why It Matters

- Inaccurate sizing and poor fit are major contributors to returns in online fashion retail more broadly, underscoring the scale of the sizing problem across the fashion industry.
- The abaya is worn for extended periods throughout the day, so a poor fit can directly affect comfort and confidence.
- Common abaya-specific sizing issues include sleeve and overall lengths that don't align with a woman's height, which standardized market sizes rarely account for.

## The Gap

Despite the growth of e-commerce and fashion technology, there is currently no widely adopted, intelligent solution that recommends abayas based on a user's **personalized body measurements or body shape**. This gap contributes to:

- Frequent sizing issues and returns,
- A shopping experience that doesn't reflect real body diversity,
- Missed opportunities to combine modern AI techniques with traditional fashion needs.

## Motivation for Hullah

Hullah was developed to fill this gap with a **smart, AI-powered application** capable of:

- Analyzing a user's measurements or a single photo,
- Accurately determining body shape, and
- Providing customized abaya recommendations that align with both **fit** and **cultural preferences**.

By doing so, Hullah aims to reduce fitting errors, save users time and money, and improve the overall abaya shopping experience.
